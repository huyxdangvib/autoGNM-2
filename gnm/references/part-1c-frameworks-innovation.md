## 2. Innovation Frameworks (35)

### disruption
- **Disruptive Innovation Theory** — How simpler, cheaper solutions overtake incumbents via overlooked segments
- **Jobs to be Done** — Uncover customer jobs and hired solutions to reveal unmet needs
- **Blue Ocean Strategy** — Create uncontested market space via value innovation (eliminate/reduce/raise/create)
- **Crossing the Chasm** — Navigate gap between early adopters and mainstream with beachhead strategy
- **Platform Revolution** — Transform linear value chains into exponential platform ecosystems

### business_model
- **Long Tail Model** — Monetize aggregated niche demand when marginal cost approaches zero
- **Business Model Canvas** — Map nine building blocks: segments, propositions, channels, revenue, resources, activities, partners, costs
- **Value Proposition Canvas** — Match customer jobs/pains/gains with precision
- **Business Model Patterns** — Apply proven patterns: subscription, freemium, marketplace, razor-blade
- **Revenue Model Innovation** — Explore: usage-based, performance-based, subscription alternatives
- **Cost Structure Innovation** — Redesign costs: eliminate, automate, outsource, flip fixed to variable

### market_analysis
- **TAM SAM SOM Analysis** — Size market: Total Addressable, Serviceable, Obtainable
- **Five Forces Analysis** — Industry structure and competitive dynamics
- **PESTLE Analysis** — Macro environmental factors: Political, Economic, Social, Tech, Legal, Environmental
- **Market Timing Assessment** — Evaluate readiness: technology maturity, customer readiness, window of opportunity
- **Competitive Positioning Map** — Visualize landscape to identify white space and differentiation

### strategic
- **Three Horizons Framework** — Balance: current business, emerging opportunities, future possibilities
- **Lean Startup Methodology** — Build-measure-learn in rapid cycles to validate assumptions
- **Innovation Ambition Matrix** — Portfolio balance: core, adjacent, transformational initiatives
- **Strategic Intent Development** — Define bold aspirational goals that stretch beyond current capabilities
- **Scenario Planning** — Explore multiple plausible futures for robust strategy building

### value_chain
- **Value Chain Analysis** — Map activities to identify where value is created and captured
- **Unbundling Analysis** — Break apart integrated chains to capture high-value components
- **Platform Ecosystem Design** — Architect multi-sided platforms with network effects
- **Make vs Buy Analysis** — Vertical integration versus outsourcing for competitive advantage
- **Partnership Strategy** — Design strategic partnerships for expanded capabilities and reach

### technology
- **Technology Adoption Lifecycle** — How innovations diffuse: innovators → early adopters → mainstream → laggards
- **S-Curve Analysis** — Identify inflection points in technology maturity for investment timing
- **Technology Roadmapping** — Plan capability evolution aligned with strategy and market timing
- **Open Innovation Strategy** — Leverage external innovation beyond internal R&D
- **Digital Transformation Framework** — Reimagine business models and operations through digital enablers

### execution
- **OKR Framework** — Align objectives with measurable key results across organization levels
- **Stage-Gate Process** — Decision gates validating progress before committing more resources
- **Agile Portfolio Management** — Iterative funding cycles with rapid reallocation based on learning
- **Investment Thesis Canvas** — Clear hypothesis per initiative with validation criteria and kill triggers

## 3. Value Perspectives (27)

### Deloitte Value Map
- **Operating Income — Volume Growth (New Sales)** — Grow via new customers, products, channels, market share
- **Operating Income — Volume Growth (Retention)** — Deepen relationships: cross-sell, up-sell, wallet share, loyalty
- **Operating Income — Price Realization (NIM)** — Optimize net interest income: asset pricing, liability, ALM
- **Operating Income — Price Realization (Fees)** — Grow non-interest: transaction fees, advisory, commissions
- **Operating Income — Credit Risk Management** — Protect income: underwriting, portfolio monitoring, loss management
- **Operating Income — Liquidity Management** — Optimize treasury/liquidity: LCR, NSFR, funding cost
- **Operating Expenses — Customer-Facing Efficiency** — Optimize marketing, sales, fulfillment costs
- **Operating Expenses — Non-Customer-Facing Efficiency** — Reduce back-office, IT, overhead via automation
- **Capital — Asset Quality and Composition** — Optimize RWA density, RORWA, portfolio diversification
- **Capital — Credit Risk Capital** — Manage EL, RWA, CAR, provision coverage
- **Capital — Market and Operational Risk Capital** — Control VaR, operational loss, capital charges
- **Expectations — MIS and Financial Control** — Decision-quality reporting, forecasting, BI
- **Expectations — Stakeholder and Regulatory** — Investor relations, regulatory reporting, governance

### Balanced Scorecard (BSC)
- **Financial — Productivity (Cost Structure)** — Eliminate waste, reduce complexity, operational leverage
- **Financial — Productivity (Asset Utilization)** — Improve utilization, capacity management, asset sweating
- **Financial — Growth (Revenue Expansion)** — New markets, new products, deeper relationships
- **Financial — Growth (Customer Value Enhancement)** — Increase willingness to pay, loyalty, wallet share
- **Customer — Product and Service Attributes** — Quality, price, availability, functionality
- **Customer — Relationships** — Trust, switching costs, referrals, engagement
- **Customer — Brand and Image** — Awareness, preference, equity, share of voice
- **Internal Process — Operations Management** — Consistent quality, speed, cost efficiency
- **Internal Process — Customer Management** — Acquisition, retention, cross-sell, service precision
- **Internal Process — Product and Innovation** — Develop/launch new products at speed
- **Internal Process — Risk and Regulatory** — Robust risk management and compliance
- **Learning & Growth — Human Capital** — Talent, skills, decision-making capability
- **Learning & Growth — Information Capital** — IT systems, data infrastructure, MIS
- **Learning & Growth — Organizational Capital** — Culture, governance, organizational design

## 4. Business Case Phases (11)

| # | Code | Phase | Description |
|---|------|-------|-------------|
| 1 | BC | Business Case | Strategic rationale, market need, financial projections, risk profile |
| 2 | PC | Product Concept | Value proposition, target segments, differentiation, scope |
| 3 | PS | Product Specification | Product parameters, eligibility criteria, customer journey, ops requirements |
| 4 | CR | Credit & Risk Policy | Risk appetite, approval authority, policy exceptions, collateral |
| 5 | PP | Pricing & Profitability | Cost structure, pricing model, profitability analysis, sensitivity |
| 6 | OP | Operations & Process | E2E process map, SOPs, checklists, SLAs |
| 7 | IT | IT & Systems | IT requirements, system params, integrations, MIS specs |
| 8 | LC | Legal/Compliance/Docs | Regulatory review, legal documentation, compliance sign-off |
| 9 | PU | Pilot & UAT | Controlled launch, user acceptance testing, defect resolution |
| 10 | LS | Launch Strategy | Go-to-market plan, rollout schedule, communication, training |
| 11 | PM | Monitoring & Variants | Post-launch KPI tracking, variant readiness, continuous improvement |

> Phases 1-7 are the universal business case framework. Phases 8-11 are **regulated industry extensions** (banking, insurance, healthcare).
